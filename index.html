Five Years Of Building What's Next

NEXUS started in 2021 as a small gathering of 200 engineers in a borrowed conference room in London. The idea was simple: get the people who are actually building technology in the same room as the people who are funding and designing it, and see what happens. What happened was three days of conversations so good that people missed their flights on purpose.

Five years later NEXUS has grown into one of the most anticipated technology conferences in the world. Not because of the production budget — though that has improved significantly since the borrowed conference room — but because of the quality of the people in the room. We are relentlessly selective about our speakers, our sponsors, and our agenda. If it isn't genuinely useful or genuinely interesting, it doesn't make the programme.

NEXUS 2026 is our first year in Lagos, and we could not be more intentional about that choice. The technology ecosystem in Africa is not emerging. It has emerged. We are here to meet it where it is.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NEXUS 2026 in person only?

Yes — NEXUS is designed as an in-person experience. Select talks will be available as recordings for Professional and Executive ticket holders after the event.

Can I get a refund on my ticket?

Full refunds are available up to 30 days before the event. After that, tickets are transferable but non-refundable.

Is there a student discount?

Yes — students with a valid institution email can apply for a 40% discount on General tickets via our contact form.

Will sessions be recorded?

All main stage sessions will be recorded and made available to Professional and Executive ticket holders within 7 days of the event.
